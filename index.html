DANGERS OF DIY

Pest control is essential for maintaining a healthy home and protecting your family from potential harm. However, many people attempt to do their own pest control, which can be dangerous. Without the proper training and equipment, you could put yourself and your family at risk of exposure to harmful chemicals or pests. Additionally, DIY pest control is generally much less effective, as the average person lacks the skill and knowledge to truly eliminate a pest infestation.

If you’re allergic to bees, wasps, or any other type of pest, then we don’t need to tell you how hazardous it can be to try dealing with an infestation on your own. Without the right protective equipment and tools, you could be seriously injured.

If you’re not knowledgeable on exactly how to treat a pest problem, it is highly unlikely that you will be able to handle your pest issue on your own. Even professional treatments require follow-up services! Keep in mind that if you’ve spotted a single ant, mouse, termite, or any other common pest, there are likely hundreds or thousands more hiding where you can’t find them.

PREVENTATIVE MEASURES


All this is not to say there’s nothing you can do on your own! Homeowners can take a few extra steps to mitigate unwanted pests in between treatments. In the end, this is a team effort; your exterminator does the pest treatments, and you can do your part to maintain them.

With the help of a pest control expert, you can locate and seal any entry points that pests might be using to infest your property. If you suspect you have a leak somewhere in your plumbing system, repair it as soon as possible; pests need a water source, so make sure they can’t easily get one. Likewise, eliminate sources of standing water in your yard. Store food in airtight containers and dispose of garbage regularly to prevent attracting scavengers. If you have a firewood pile, keep it well away from your home, and haul away yard debris promptly; it’s vital to eliminate places for pests to nest. If they don’t have a home base, it’s harder for them to establish a presence on your property.
